What it actually is
More than a balance.
It's a read on your pace.

Your bank shows you a number. But a number alone doesn't tell you if that number is fine for a Tuesday or already stressed for a Friday. Tide adds the one thing a balance is missing: context.

Set a budget for any period — a week, a vacation, a month — and Tide gives you a living gauge of where you stand relative to how much time is left. One glance, and you know.

🌊

Pace-aware, not just balance-aware

$200 left on Monday feels different from $200 left on Saturday. Tide accounts for where you are in the period — so the read is always honest, never misleading.

🧭

For free cash flow, not just savings

Most money tools push you to save every dollar. Tide is for the money you've already decided to spend. It helps you pace it well, so you don't run out before the week does.

☀️

Resets with optimism built in

Every new period, the tide comes back in. There's no accumulated guilt, no running score of past mistakes — just a fresh read on right now.

The idea behind it

The tide always
comes back in.

Most financial tools frame spending as failure. Every dollar that leaves your account is subtracted from some implied ideal, and the UI makes sure you feel it.

Tide works from a different premise: spending is part of life, not a problem to solve. You've budgeted for it. The gauge just shows you how you're moving through it — like a navigator checking the chart, not a parent checking a report card.

And when the period resets? The tide comes back in. Full. No judgment, no accumulated guilt. Just a fresh reading and a new week ahead.

🌊

High tide — you've got room

Deep water. Your budget is full, or close to it. Spend comfortably. This is what it's for.

🏄

Mid tide — on pace

You're moving through the budget at roughly the right speed for where you are in the week. Good.

🪨

Low tide — be thoughtful

Not a warning. Just an honest read. The rocks are showing — worth knowing before you commit to something.

🌅

Reset — the tide comes in

New period, full budget, fresh start. The tide returns the same way it always has. Built-in optimism.
